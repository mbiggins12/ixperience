About

I am a class of 2016 economics student studying at the University of Notre Dame. I am originally from the Midwest, Kansas City. However, during the past 3 years I have been privileged to gain perspectives from Silicon Valley, Cape Town, & D.C.

I have a passion for learning about the interplay between business and innovation and the positive impact it can have on society. My goal in life is to implement advancing technologies with the necessary business forethought to create impactful economic and societal benefits.

Coffee for a Cure is a program run by Notre Dame's residential community allowing students to financially empower science research on campus by purchasing cost-competitive K-Cups, with a portion of each sale benefiting our research.

Ampush Philanthropy:
Giving back with facebook

The Ampush media team has partnered with the Raphael House of San Francisco to amplify its marketing objectives: acquire user signups for its Adopt a Family Program as well as collect donations for the organization. By leveraging the power of Facebook ad types, Ampush and the Raphael House hope to use Facebook for good and revolutionize the way charities achieve their goals and objectives.
